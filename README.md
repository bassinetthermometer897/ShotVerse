## 📋 What is ShotVerse?

ShotVerse is a software tool that helps you create cinematic video shots using text instructions. You type what you want to see, and ShotVerse plans and controls the camera automatically. This lets you make multi-shot videos without complex editing or camera setups.

The program breaks the task into two parts. First, it plans the camera moves based on your text. Then it controls the camera to create smooth and precise shots. This makes it easier to get the exact video you want.

---

## 🎯 Who is this for?

ShotVerse works well for people who want to create videos with interesting camera angles but don’t have advanced equipment or skills. Filmmakers, students, or content creators can use it to try new ideas quickly.

You do not need to know programming or video editing to use ShotVerse. The software guides you step-by-step from installation to running your first video.

## 2. Install ShotVerse 🖥️

1. Find the downloaded file in your Downloads folder. It will be named something like `ShotVerse-Setup.exe`.
2. Double-click the file to start the installer.
3. Follow the prompts on screen:
   - Agree to the license terms.
   - Choose the installation folder or accept the default.
   - Click “Install” to start copying files.
4. Once finished, click “Finish” to exit the installer.

You can now find ShotVerse in your Start menu or on your desktop.

---

## 3. Opening ShotVerse and Setting Up 🏁

1. Launch ShotVerse from the Start menu or desktop shortcut.
2. The first time you run it, it will download some extra data needed to work. Make sure your internet is on.
3. Wait for this download to finish. It usually takes a few minutes.
4. After setup, the main screen will appear with a simple text box and options to start creating.

---

## 4. Creating Your First Text-Driven Video 🎥

1. In the main window, you will see a box labeled “Enter your shot description.”
2. Type a simple instruction, like:  
   *“Show a wide shot of a mountain, then zoom in slowly to a tent.”*
3. Click the “Plan and Run” button.
4. ShotVerse will take a moment to generate the camera moves based on your text.
5. You can watch the preview video on screen.
6. If you want to save the video, click “Export” to store it to your computer.
7. Repeat with other descriptions to shoot different scenes.

---

## 5. Adjusting Camera Behavior ⚙️

ShotVerse allows some basic control over the camera’s speed and angles. Use the controls on the right side of the window:

- **Speed:** Move the slider to change how fast the camera moves.
- **Angle:** Select presets like “Wide,” “Medium,” or “Close-up.”
- **Smoothness:** Adjust how smooth or sharp the transitions are.

Try different settings to see what fits your scene best.

---

## 6. Viewing and Managing Your Videos 📂

All videos you create are saved in the default folder:  

`Documents\ShotVerse\Videos`

You can open this folder anytime from the main menu under “Open Videos Folder.”

From here, you can share your videos, upload them to other platforms, or edit them with other software.

---

## 7. Troubleshooting Basics 🛠️

If ShotVerse does not start or crashes:

- Make sure your Windows is up to date.
- Check that you have enough free disk space.
- Restart your computer and try again.
- Disable antivirus temporarily if it blocks installation or running.
- Check your internet connection during the initial setup.

If videos become jerky, slower, or behave oddly:

- Lower the camera speed setting.
- Close other heavy programs running in the background.
- Increase smoothness slider.
